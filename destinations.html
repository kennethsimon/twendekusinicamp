    DESTINATIONS

    SELOUS GAME RESERVE

    The largest wilderness area in Africa covers an area of 50,000sq km. The reserve supports an enormous number of elephants, buffalos and wilderbeasts, hippos, crocoldiles, over 2000 variety of plants, hot volcanic springs, lagoons and over 440 species of birds, other rare species including the african hunting dogs. Its lifeline comes from the Great Rufiji River which meanders through the park from its head waters in the Ruaha valley to the river delta opposite the Mafia Island. This world Heritage site can be explored on foot, boat or vehicle.

    RUAHA NATIONAL PARK

    Tanzania`s biggest park and host to a number of animals which include the African Wild Dog, roan and sable antelope and the greater kudu. The Great Ruaha river that provides life to the park, flows through the eastern side of the park and its home to hippos, crocodiles and a variety of water birds. The vegetation in Ruaha is mainly miombo woodlands, baobabs and acacia trees.
